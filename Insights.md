# Key Insights
1. **Global Life Expectancy Improved Over Time**
Life expectancy showed a generally upward trend between 2000 and 2015, indicating improvements in healthcare access, disease prevention, and living conditions across many countries.

2. **Developed Countries Recorded Higher Life Expectancy**
   Developed countries consistently outperformed developing countries in life expectancy, highlighting persistent inequalities in health outcomes and access to resources.
3. **Adult Mortality Was Strongly Associated with Lower Life Expectancy**
Countries with higher adult mortality rates tended to have significantly lower life expectancy, suggesting that premature deaths remain a major barrier to longevity.
4. **Education Was Positively Associated with Longevity**
Countries with higher average years of schooling generally recorded higher life expectancy, emphasizing the role of education in improving health awareness and quality of life.
5. **Economic Development Supports Longer Lifespans**
GDP and Income Composition of Resources showed positive relationships with life expectancy, indicating that economic prosperity contributes to better health outcomes.
6. **HIV/AIDS Remains a Major Health Challenge**
Higher HIV/AIDS rates were associated with lower life expectancy, demonstrating the significant impact of infectious diseases on population health.
7. **Child Health Is Closely Linked to Life Expectancy**
Countries with higher under-five mortality and higher rates of child thinness generally experienced lower life expectancy, highlighting the importance of early-life health interventions.
8. **Healthcare Investment and Vaccination Matter**
Countries with stronger healthcare investment and higher vaccination coverage generally achieved better life expectancy outcomes.

# Recommendations
- Increase investment in healthcare infrastructure.
- Expand access to quality education.
- Strengthen vaccination programs.
- Improve child nutrition and maternal health services.
- Continue efforts to reduce HIV/AIDS-related mortality.
- Prioritize interventions in countries with high adult mortality.
